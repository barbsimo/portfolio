Mushrooms Found Fall 2016

My first season as an amature mycologist (so believe identification at your own risk, but I am still here to tell.)

King Bolete (Boletus edulis)
Found in woods at about 3000 to 4000 feet in fall. Spores under cap (verses gills). "Many connoisseurs regarded this mushroom as the best, something I wouldn't argue about. I just love the nutty taste and consistency of this impressive mushroom."
Pacific Golden Chanterelle (Cantharellus formosus)
The Golden chanterelle is the most abundant of all coastal chanterelles in the Pacific Northwest. There is no other place on this globe that offers a comparable chanterelles bounty as does the Pacific Northwest. This is Chanterelle heaven, no doubt!
Shaggy Parasol.
Found in many forests and backyards. Some say it's the most abundant tasty backyard mushroom in the PNW. A word of advice: cook and eat it soon after harvesting. It ages quickly after picking.
Winter Chanterelle (Craterellus tubaeformis)
Found a bit later in the year - hence it's name. Grows in mossy areas. If you find one there are likely many more around, which is god because it's small and you'll want a lot of them! As with most chenterelles, dry fry it a bit before adding butter or olive oil. Much more palateable / less slimey.
Matsutake or Pine Mushroom (Tricholoma magnielare)
One test for identifying this mushroom is the "wiggle test". Give it a push and notice how solid it is - does not wiggle. If you are fortunate and find this mushroom remember where the location becuase it will fruit for many years in the same place.
